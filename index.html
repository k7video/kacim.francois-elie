    About my research

    I am currently investigating the DPN acceleration method applied to the method of the characteristics (MOC) for solving the BTE for neutrons. This research is related to nuclear reactor physics, specifically lattice calculations.
    The MOC consists in the resolution of a PDE along characteristic curves. In Fig. (a) a set of characteristics is represented, for two directions on a domain D. Solving along a characteristic curve reduces the PDE to an ODE, which is useful for a bunch of reasons.

    When using numerical methods, one often use a mesh to partition the domain. When it comes to MOC, there is a transition relation for the flux between two adjacent cell of a mesh, which allow a sweep, as illustrated in Fig. (b).

    In nuclear reactor physics, the domain of resolution for the BTE is the reactor core is usually very big. For this reason, computations are very often divided in two separate steps: the lattice computation and the core computation.
    The lattice step is made on small parts of the domain (pin cell, assemblies of pins in Fig. (c)), and the core step is made on the whole core, while using the results obtained on the previous step.
